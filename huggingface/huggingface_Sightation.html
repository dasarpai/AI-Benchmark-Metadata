      Often, the needs and visual abilities differ between the annotator group and the end user group. Generating detailed diagram descriptions for blind and low-vision (BLV) users is one such challenging domain. Sighted annotators could describe visuals with ease, but existing studies have shown that direct generations by them are costly, bias-prone, and somewhat lacking by BLV standards. In this study, we ask sighted individuals to assessâ€”rather than produceâ€”diagram descriptions generated by vision-language models (VLM) that have been guided with latent supervision via a multi-pass inference. The sighted assessments prove effective and useful to professional educators who are themselves BLV and teach visually impaired learners. We release SIGHTATION, a collection of diagram description datasets spanning 5k diagrams and 137k samples for completion, preference, retrieval, question answering, and reasoning training purposes and demonstrate their fine-tuning potential in various downstream tasks.

      About SightationPreference

      SightationPreference is a preference dataset consisting of 1k diagrams and 10k chosen-rejected description samples. We constructed the chosen-rejected pairs from three contenders.

      • In-model Contenders

        • The sighted annotators chose a preferred description from 1k pairs of 2 different diagram descriptions: basic description and guide-generated description. Two descriptions were generated by GPT4o-mini and Qwen2-VL-72B, resulting in 1k*2=2k chosen-rejected pairs.

      • Cross-model Contenders

        • Within each of the 4 cross-model comparisons (e.g., basic description from GPT VS guide-generated description from Qwen), the winner was chosen using the rated score annotated by the sighted users, resulting in 1k*4=4k chosen-rejected pairs.

      • Synthetic Contenders

        • With the 4k descriptions generated by GPT and Qwen, we synthesized an inferior (â€œrejectedâ€) description by removing its best sentence, which was annotated by the sighted users. We removed a random non-best sentence from the original description and label to synthesize the â€œchosenâ€ description, resulting in 4k chosen-rejected pairs.
