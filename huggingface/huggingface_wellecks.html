Carathéodory's Theorem (Analysis)
Let $I \subseteq \R$.\nLet $c \in I$ be an [[Definition:Interior Point|interior point]] of $I$. {{Disambiguate|Definition:Interior Point}} {{explain|In this case, there appears not to be a definition for "interior point" which appropriately captures the gist of this. === Necessary Condition ===\nSuppose $f$ is [[Definition:Differentiable Real Function at Point|differentiable]] at $c$.\nThen by definition $f' \left({c}\right)$ exists.\nSo we can define $\varphi$ by: :$\varphi \left({x}\right) = \begin{cases} \dfrac {f \left({x}\right) - f \left({c}\right)} {x - c} & : x \ne c, x \in I \\ f' \left({c}\right) & : x = c \end{cases}$\nCondition $(2)$, that $\varphi$ is [[Definition:Continuous Real Function at Point|continuous]] at $c$, is satisfied, since: {{begin-eqn}} {{eqn | l = \varphi \left({c}\right) | r = f' \left({c}\right) | c = Definition of $\varphi$ }}\n{{eqn | r = \lim_{x \to c} \frac {f \left({x}\right) - f \left({c}\right)} {x - c} | c = Definition of [[Definition:Derivative|derivative]] }}\n{{eqn | r = \lim_{x \to c} \varphi \left({x}\right) | c = Definition of $\varphi$ }} {{end-eqn}}\nFinally, condition $(1)$ is vacuous for $x = c$.\nFor $x \ne c$, it follows from the definition of $\varphi$ by dividing both sides of $(1)$ by $x - c$.\n{{qed|lemma}}\n=== Sufficient Condition ===\nSuppose a $\varphi$ as in the theorem statement exists.\nThen for $x \ne c$, we have that: :$\varphi \left({x}\right) = \dfrac {f \left({x}\right) - f \left({c}\right)} {x - c}$\nSince $\varphi$ is [[Definition:Continuous Real Function at Point|continuous]] at $c$: :$\displaystyle \varphi \left({c}\right) = \lim_{x \to c} \varphi \left({x}\right) = \lim_{x \to c} \frac {f \left({x}\right) - f \left({c}\right)} {x - c}$\nThat is, $f$ is [[Definition:Differentiable Real Function at Point|differentiable]] at $c$, and $f' \left({c}\right) = \varphi \left({c}\right)$.\n{{qed}} {{Namedfor|Constantin Carathéodory|cat = Carathéodory}} [[Category:Direct Proofs]] [[Category:Real Analysis]] [[Category:Differential Calculus]] kuvgsqeze4423l7w2tclrn6y4dqellz

One-Step Subgroup Test
Let $\struct {G, \circ}$ be a [[Definition:Group|group]].\nLet $H$ be a [[Definition:Subset|subset]] of $G$.\nThen $\struct {H, \circ}$ is a [[Definition:Subgroup|subgroup]] of $\struct {G, \circ}$ {{iff}}: : $(1): \quad H \ne \O$, that is, $H$ is [[Definition:Non-Empty Set|non-empty]] : $(2): \quad \forall a, b \in H: a \circ b^{-1} \in H$.
=== Necessary Condition ===\nLet $H$ be a [[Definition:Subset|subset]] of $G$ that fulfils the conditions given.\nIt is noted that the fact that $H$ is [[Definition:Non-Empty Set|non-empty]] is one of the conditions.\nIt is also noted that the [[Definition:Group Operation|group operation]] of $\struct {H, \circ}$ is the same as that for $\struct {G, \circ}$, that is, $\circ$.\nSo it remains to show that $\struct {H, \circ}$ is a [[Definition:Group|group]].\nWe check the four [[Definition:Group Axioms|group axioms]]: '''$\text G 1$: Associativity''':\nFrom [[Subset Product within Semigroup is Associative]], [[Definition:Associative Algebraic Structure|associativity]] is inherited by $\struct {H, \circ}$ from $\struct {G, \circ}$. '''$\text G 2$: Identity'''\nLet $e$ be the [[Definition:Identity Element|identity]] of $\struct {G, \circ}$.\nSince $H$ is [[Definition:Non-Empty Set|non-empty]], $\exists x \in H$.\nIf we take $a = x$ and $b = x$, then $a \circ b^{-1} = x \circ x^{-1} = e \in H$, where $e$ is the [[Definition:Identity Element|identity element]]. '''$\text G 3$: Inverses'''\nIf we take $a = e$ and $b = x$, then $a \circ b^{-1} = e \circ x^{-1} = x^{-1} \in H$.\nThus every element of $H$ has an [[Definition:Inverse Element|inverse]] also in $H$. '''$\text G 0$: Closure'''\nLet $x, y \in H$.\nThen $y^{-1} \in H$, so we may take $a = x$ and $b = y^{-1}$.\nSo: :$a \circ b^{-1} = x \circ \paren {y^{-1} }^{-1} = x \circ y \in H$\nThus, $H$ is [[Definition:Closed Algebraic Structure|closed]].\nTherefore, $\struct {H, \circ}$ satisfies all the [[Definition:Group Axioms|group axioms]], and is therefore a [[Definition:Group|group]].\nTherefore $\struct {H, \circ}$ is a [[Definition:Subgroup|subgroup]] of $\struct {G, \circ}$.\n{{qed|lemma}}\n=== Sufficient Condition ===\nNow suppose $\struct {H, \circ}$ is a [[Definition:Subgroup|subgroup]] of $\struct {G, \circ}$. : $(1): \quad H \le G \implies H \ne \O$ from the fact that $H$ is a [[Definition:Group|group]] and therefore [[Group is not Empty|can not be empty]]. : $(2): \quad$ As $\struct {H, \circ}$ is a [[Definition:Group|group]], it is [[Definition:Closed Algebraic Structure|closed]] and every element has an [[Definition:Inverse Element|inverse]]. So it follows that $\forall a, b \in H: a \circ b^{-1} \in H$.\n{{qed}}
