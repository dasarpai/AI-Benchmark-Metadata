                        Fudeno-Instruct-4M: Multimodal SVG Generation Dataset

                        Fudeno-Instruct-4M is a comprehensive dataset containing over 4 million rows of multimodal data designed to train models for understanding and generating SVG graphics from natural language instructions. To our knowledge, this is the largest dataset of its kind, combining both synthetic and high-quality real-world examples.

                        Dataset Overview

                        The dataset contains:

                        • Over 3 million examples with complete SVG-prompt-image triads
                        • A diverse range of SVG complexity from simple shapes to intricate designs
                        • Carefully balanced distribution of synthetic and curated examples
                        • Comprehensive train/validation/test splits for model development

                        Data Composition

                        Fudeno-Instruct-4M merges two distinct datasets:

                        1. Random Generated SVGs (25% of final dataset)

                        • Generated using a high-performance Golang script
                        • Systematic variation in complexity, elements, and styling
                        • Synthetic captions derived directly from SVG structure
                        • Enables models to learn fundamental SVG generation principles
                        • Source ID: takara-ai/rand-1m-multimodal

                        2. High-Quality Curated SVGs (75% of final dataset)

                        Dataset Creation Process

                        1. Data Acquisition

                          • Random SVGs generated programmatically with systematic variation
                          • Quality SVGs collected from open source repositories

                        2. Caption Generation

                          • Random SVGs: Synthetic captions created from structural analysis
                          • Quality SVGs: Captions generated using vision-language model and NLP techniques

                        3. Dataset Concatenation

                          • Column alignment across both sources
                          • Deduplication of exact matches
                          • Quality-focused 75%/25% distribution weighting

                        4. Split Creation

                          • Train (80%), validation (10%), and test (10%) splits
                          • Stratified splitting to ensure similar complexity distribution

                        • Multimodal understanding of vector graphics
