Explain the difference between supply and demand theory in microeconomics and macroeconomics.
Microeconomics: In microeconomics, supply and demand theory focuses on individual markets for specific goods and services. It analyzes how supply and demand determines the price and quantity of a particular product. The interaction of supply and demand for one product in isolation is studied. Supply is determined by individual producers and depends on production costs and prices of related goods. Demand is determined by individual consumers and depends on consumer preferences, income, and prices of substitutes and complements. When supply and demand are balanced in a market, the equilibrium price and quantity are achieved. If supply exceeds demand, prices tend to fall. If demand exceeds supply, prices tend to rise. The goal is efficient allocation of resources at the market level. Macroeconomics: In macroeconomics, supply and demand theory looks at the overall output and income in an economy and the aggregated demands and supplies of all goods and services. It studies how supply and demand affects employment, inflation, and economic growth for the whole economy. Aggregate supply depends on factors like labor force, productivity, technology, and prices of inputs. Aggregate demand depends on consumption, investment, government spending and net exports in the economy. When aggregate supply and demand are balanced at a macroeconomic level, the economy reaches full employment and potential GDP. If aggregate demand exceeds supply, inflationary pressures build up. If supply exceeds demand, there is recession or unemployment. The goal is efficient allocation of resources at the economy level.

Explain the basic components of supply and demand and how they determine price in a market economy, including the effects of government intervention and market failures that may distort prices from what would occur in a free market.
Supply: The quantity of a good or service that producers are willing to provide at different price levels during a given time period. Supply is determined by production costs, technology, and producer expectations. As the price of a product rises, suppliers typically are willing to produce and sell more of it. This is because they can cover their costs and make a profit at the higher price. The relationship between price and quantity supplied is shown in the supply curve. Demand: The quantity of a good or service that consumers are willing and able to purchase at different prices during a given time period. Demand is determined by consumer preferences, incomes, prices of substitute goods, and population size. As the price of a product falls, consumers typically want to buy more of it. The relationship between price and quantity demanded is shown in the demand curve. Price Determination: Where the supply and demand curves intersect is the equilibrium price and quantity in a free market. At this point, the quantity demanded by consumers equals the quantity supplied by producers. The price aims to balance the interests of buyers and sellers.
